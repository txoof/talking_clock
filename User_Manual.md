# Talking Clock User Manual

## Quick Start

**To get started:**

1. Plug the USB cable into the round port on the back of the clock, then connect it to a USB power adapter.
2. The clock will start up and announce the current time within a few seconds.
3. You may need to adjust the time.
4. Press the large button on top at any time to hear the time announced again.

That is all you need to know to use the clock. The rest of this manual describes how to adjust the volume, set the time, configure the alarm, and change other settings.

> **Note:** The time shown at startup comes from a small battery-powered clock inside the device. If the clock is new, or the internal battery was replaced, the time may need to be set. See *Setting the Time* below.

---

## Getting to Know Your Clock

The clock has three buttons.

**The Announce button** is the large round button on top of the clock. Press it at any time to hear the current time.

**The Plus button (+)** is a square button on the back of the clock, on the right side near the power cable. It is used to increase volume and navigate settings.

**The Minus button (-)** is a round button on the back of the clock. It is used to decrease volume and navigate settings.

The **USB power port** is on the back of the clock, next to the Plus button. Use any standard USB phone charger and cable. The clock does not have an on/off switch; it runs whenever it is plugged in.

There is a **SD card slot** on the left side of the clock. The SD card holds the clock's voice files and should be left in place during normal use.

The clock is made from laser-cut birch wood and is held together with four slotted bolts on the lid.

---

## Adjusting the Volume

With the clock in normal use, a short press of the Plus button raises the volume and a short press of the Minus button lowers it. Each press plays a brief tone so you can hear the change. There are 20 volume steps. The default is step 9. There is no spoken warning at the minimum or maximum; the tone simply stops changing in character.

Volume is saved automatically a few seconds after you stop adjusting it.

---

## Setting the Time

1. Hold either the Plus or Minus button for about 1.5 seconds. when you release the button, the clock says *"settings"*.
2. Press Plus or Minus briefly to scroll through the menu until you hear *"set time"*.
3. Press the Announce button to select it.
4. The clock will speak the current hour. Press Plus or Minus to change the hour. Each short press moves one hour and the clock speaks the new hour aloud.
5. When the hour is correct, press the Announce button to confirm it and move on to minutes.
6. The clock will speak the current minute. Press Plus or Minus to change the minute. Short presses move one minute at a time and are spoken aloud. Holding a button will step through minutes quickly with a beep for each step; when you release the button, the current minute or hour is spoken.
7. Press the Announce button to confirm and save the new time. The clock will announce the time you have set.

> **Tip:** If you stop interacting with the menu for about 30 seconds, the clock will say *"done"* and return to normal operation saving all the changes.

## The Settings Menu

Hold either the Plus or Minus button for about 1.5 seconds to open the settings menu. The clock will say *"settings"* and then announce the first item. Use short presses of Plus and Minus to move between items. Press the Announce button to select or change the current item. To exit the menu at any time, hold any button for 1.5 seconds; the clock will say *"done"*.

The menu items are:

- **Set time** - Set the current hour and minute.
- **Set alarm** - Set the alarm time. Uses the same hour and minute entry process as setting the time.
- **Alarm on/off** - Toggles the alarm between enabled and disabled.
- **Alarm tone** - Cycles through available alarm tones. The index number is announced, followed by a short preview of the tone played three times.
- **Announcement interval** - Sets the clock to announce the time automatically. Options are: off, every hour, every half hour, and every quarter hour. Off by default.
- **Time format** - Selects the speaking mode. See *Speaking Modes* below.
- **Voice** - Selects the voice and language. See *Voices and Languages* below.

---

## The Alarm

When the alarm is enabled and the set time is reached, the clock plays the selected tone three times, then announces the current time. It repeats this cycle every few seconds for up to five minutes. After five minutes, the alarm will be automatically silenced.

To silence the alarm, press the Announce button. The clock will announce the time and return to normal operation.

The alarm must be both set to the correct time and switched on to activate. Enabling the alarm is a separate step from setting the alarm time.

---

## Speaking Modes

The clock supports four modes for announcing the time. Each one reflects a different context and level of formality. You can change the mode in the settings menu under *"time format"*.

**Operational** is the style used in the military and aviation, where there is no ambiguity about AM or PM. Time is always expressed in 24-hour terms.

**Broadcast** is the style used in airports, train stations, and news programmes. AM or PM is always stated explicitly.

**Standard** is the default. It is the style used in offices and everyday professional settings. Practical and unambiguous without being overly formal.

**Casual** is conversational. It uses expressions like "quarter past" and "half past" in the way you might speak among friends.

| Time    | Mode        | Spoken                    |
|---------|-------------|---------------------------|
| 8:30 AM | Operational | oh eight thirty           |
| 8:45 PM | Operational | twenty forty-five         |
| 9:27 AM | Broadcast   | nine twenty-seven a.m.    |
| 6:15 PM | Broadcast   | six fifteen p.m.          |
| 4:00 PM | Standard    | four o'clock              |
| 7:12 AM | Casual      | quarter past seven        |
| 9:28 PM | Casual      | half past nine            |

## Voices and Languages

The clock ships with several voice options in English and Dutch. Voices are selected in the settings menu under *"voice"*. When you cycle to a voice, the clock announces its name in that voice so you can hear how it sounds before confirming.

Additional voices and languages can be added to the SD card. The SD card is located on the left side of the clock. For instructions on creating and installing voice packages, refer to the project documentation.

---

## The Internal Battery

The clock contains a small battery that keeps the time accurate when the USB power is disconnected. This battery is separate from the USB power supply and is user-serviceable. If the clock loses track of the time when unplugged, the battery may need replacement. The battery is located inside the enclosure; the lid is held on by four slotted bolts.
